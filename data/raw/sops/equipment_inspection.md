# Routine Equipment Inspection and Health Assessment

## 1. Purpose
This procedure establishes standardized inspection protocols for routine equipment health assessment to identify potential failures before they occur.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all rotating equipment, motors, conveyors, compressors, pumps, and auxiliary systems requiring periodic inspection.

## 3. Inspection Frequencies

### Daily Inspections (Operators)
- Visual walkaround of all operating equipment
- Check for unusual noise or vibration
- Verify normal operating temperatures
- Inspect for leaks (oil, water, air)
- Confirm proper operation of cooling systems
- Duration: 15-20 minutes per area

### Weekly Inspections (Technicians)
- Detailed visual inspection
- Basic measurements (temperature, vibration)
- Lubrication level checks
- Belt tension and wear inspection
- Connection tightness verification
- Duration: 30-45 minutes per critical asset

### Monthly Inspections (Maintenance Team)
- Comprehensive condition assessment
- Vibration analysis with instruments
- Thermographic scanning
- Electrical testing (current, voltage, insulation)
- Alignment checks
- Detailed documentation and trending
- Duration: 1-2 hours per critical asset

### Quarterly Inspections (Reliability Team)
- In-depth analysis and diagnostics
- Oil sampling and analysis
- Predictive maintenance assessment
- Equipment performance evaluation
- RUL (Remaining Useful Life) estimation
- Duration: 2-4 hours per critical asset

## 4. Daily Operator Inspection Checklist

### General Equipment Walkthrough
**Inspection Route**: Follow designated path covering all critical equipment

**Visual Inspection Points:**
- [ ] Equipment running smoothly (no jerking or hesitation)
- [ ] No unusual sounds (grinding, squealing, knocking)
- [ ] No excessive vibration (can be felt by hand)
- [ ] No hot spots (use hand test, no direct contact)
- [ ] No visible leaks (oil, grease, water, air)
- [ ] No smoke or burning smell
- [ ] Guards and covers in place
- [ ] Emergency stop accessible and functional

**Housekeeping Check:**
- [ ] Area clean and free of debris
- [ ] No oil spills or slip hazards
- [ ] Fire extinguisher accessible
- [ ] Adequate lighting
- [ ] Ventilation adequate

**Action on Abnormality:**
- Minor issue → Log in operator notes, inform supervisor
- Moderate issue → Create maintenance work request
- Severe issue → Emergency stop, call maintenance immediately

## 5. Weekly Technician Inspection

### Motor Inspection Protocol

**Visual Assessment:**
1. **Motor Exterior**
   - Check for oil/grease accumulation
   - Inspect cooling fan for damage or obstruction
   - Verify motor mounting bolts tight
   - Check terminal box cover secured
   - Look for signs of overheating (discoloration)

2. **Ventilation System**
   - Clean cooling fins if dusty
   - Verify fan rotation direction
   - Check air inlet/outlet clearances (minimum 150mm)
   - Remove any debris blocking airflow

3. **Connections**
   - Inspect cable entry for damage
   - Check terminal tightness (hand tight, no movement)
   - Look for signs of arcing or burning
   - Verify grounding connection solid

**Measurements:**
- **Temperature** (infrared thermometer):
  - Bearing housings: 40-80°C normal
  - Motor body: 60-90°C normal
  - Record readings if >normal or trending up

- **Vibration** (handheld meter):
  - Drive end bearing: <7 mm/s acceptable
  - Non-drive end bearing: <7 mm/s acceptable
  - Record readings and compare to baseline

- **Current Draw** (clamp meter while running):
  - Compare to nameplate FLA
  - Should be 85-105% of rated current
  - Check balance across all three phases (<10% imbalance)

**Documentation:**
- Record findings in inspection log
- Flag any values outside normal range
- Create work order for corrective action if needed

### Bearing Inspection

**Visual Check:**
- Bearing housing condition (cracks, corrosion)
- Seals intact and not leaking
- Grease relief port clear
- Mounting secure

**Touch Test** (when equipment cool):
- Feel for abnormal heat (should be warm, not hot)
- Check for excessive vibration by hand
- Listen with screwdriver-to-ear method for roughness

**Lubrication Status:**
- Check grease level in sight glass (if equipped)
- Verify last lubrication date on tag
- Add grease if approaching lubrication interval

**Red Flags:**
- Temperature >80°C → Investigate immediately
- Excessive noise → Plan bearing replacement
- Grease leakage → Check seal, verify grease type
- Vibration felt by hand → Perform vibration analysis

### Belt-Driven Systems

**Belt Inspection:**
- [ ] Belt wear (cracks, fraying, glazing)
- [ ] Belt tension (thumb pressure method: 10-15mm deflection)
- [ ] Belt alignment (visually straight on pulleys)
- [ ] Belt tracking (no edge wear)
- [ ] No oil contamination

**Pulley Inspection:**
- [ ] Pulley grooves not worn or damaged
- [ ] Set screws tight
- [ ] No wobble or runout
- [ ] Shaft shoulder contact (for tapered bushings)

**Guard Inspection:**
- [ ] Guard in place and secure
- [ ] No cracks or sharp edges
- [ ] Adequate clearance to rotating parts

**Adjustment Criteria:**
- Tension too loose → Slippage, squealing, edge wear
- Tension too tight → Bearing overload, shortened belt life
- Misalignment → One-sided wear, tracking issues
- Replace belt if: >3 cracks per 100mm, fraying, >10% elongation

### Conveyor Inspection

**Belt Condition:**
- Surface wear and cracking
- Edge damage or fraying
- Splice integrity (no separation)
- Belt tracking (centered on idlers)
- Belt tension (no sagging between idlers)

**Idlers and Rollers:**
- Rotation free and smooth (no stuck idlers)
- Bearing noise (spin by hand, listen for roughness)
- Idler alignment (perpendicular to belt travel)
- Buildup or contamination

**Structure:**
- Frame alignment and levelness
- Support brackets tight
- Walkway and handrails secure
- Chute wear and condition

**Drive System:**
- Motor and gearbox inspection (as per motor section)
- Chain/coupling condition
- Lubrication levels in gearbox
- Backstop function (if equipped)

## 6. Monthly Detailed Inspection

### Vibration Analysis

**Measurement Points:**
- Motor drive end (horizontal, vertical, axial)
- Motor non-drive end (horizontal, vertical, axial)
- Pump/fan bearing locations
- Gearbox input and output

**Analysis:**
- Record overall RMS velocity (mm/s)
- Capture FFT spectrum if analyzer capable
- Compare to baseline and previous readings
- Identify peak frequencies and match to defects
- Calculate vibration trending rate

**Severity Assessment (ISO 10816):**
- <2.8 mm/s: Good
- 2.8-7.1 mm/s: Acceptable
- 7.1-11.2 mm/s: Unsatisfactory (plan maintenance)
- >11.2 mm/s: Unacceptable (shutdown)

### Thermographic Inspection

**Scan Pattern:**
- Electrical panels and connections
- Motor winding temperature
- Bearing housings
- Couplings and flexible elements
- Cables under load

**Temperature Difference (ΔT) Criteria:**
- <15°C: Normal
- 15-40°C: Monitor (minor issue)
- 40-80°C: Alert (plan repair within 1 week)
- >80°C: Critical (immediate action)

**Thermal Patterns:**
- Hot spot on motor winding → Phase imbalance or winding fault
- Hot bearing → Lubrication issue or bearing defect
- Hot cable termination → Loose connection
- Hot contactor → Pitting or overload

### Electrical Testing (Motors De-energized)

**Insulation Resistance (Megger Test):**
- Test voltage: 500V DC for motors up to 690V AC
- Test each phase to ground and phase-to-phase
- Minimum acceptable: 1 MΩ per kV + 1 MΩ
- For 400V motor: Minimum 1.5 MΩ
- If <1 MΩ → Motor drying or rewind required

**Winding Resistance:**
- Measure resistance between phases (U-V, V-W, W-U)
- Should be balanced within 5%
- High resistance → Poor connection or open turns
- Low resistance → Short circuit

**Ground Continuity:**
- Verify <0.1 Ω resistance from motor frame to ground
- Check bonding strap integrity
- Test grounding conductor size adequate

### Alignment Check (Laser or Dial Indicator)

**When to Check:**
- After bearing or coupling replacement
- If vibration elevated at 2× RPM
- Annually for critical equipment

**Tolerance (for 1800 RPM motor):**
- Angular misalignment: <0.05 mm offset per 100mm diameter
- Parallel offset: <0.25 mm
- Shaft end float: <0.5 mm

**Correction:**
- Adjust motor position using shims
- Re-torque mounting bolts
- Re-measure until within specification
- Document final "as-left" values

## 7. Quarterly Reliability Assessment

### Oil Analysis (Gearboxes, Hydraulics)

**Sampling Procedure:**
- Take sample while equipment running (if possible)
- Use clean sampling bottle
- Label with equipment ID, date, oil hours
- Send to laboratory within 48 hours

**Key Parameters:**
- Viscosity (should be ±10% of specification)
- Particle count (ISO cleanliness code)
- Water content (<200 ppm acceptable)
- Wear metals (Fe, Cu, Al, Cr - track trends)
- Additives (check for depletion)

**Action Based on Results:**
- Normal → Continue monitoring
- Marginal → Increase sampling frequency
- Abnormal → Filter, top-up, or change oil
- Critical → Investigate for component wear

### Performance Testing

**Motor Efficiency Check:**
- Measure input power (kW)
- Calculate output power (load × speed)
- Efficiency = Output / Input × 100%
- Compare to nameplate efficiency
- Significant drop → Internal losses increasing

**Pump Performance:**
- Flow rate (using flow meter)
- Discharge pressure
- Suction pressure
- Calculate pump efficiency
- Compare to baseline curve
- Degradation → Wear, cavitation, or impeller damage

**Compressor Performance:**
- Discharge pressure
- Discharge temperature
- Flow rate (if measured)
- Power consumption
- Calculate specific power (kW per CFM)
- Trending indicates valve leakage or wear

### Equipment Health Score

**Condition-Based Scoring:**
Assign points (0-10) for each parameter:
- Vibration level
- Temperature
- Oil condition
- Electrical parameters
- Visual condition
- Performance efficiency

**Overall Health Score:**
- 90-100: Excellent (no action)
- 70-89: Good (monitor)
- 50-69: Fair (plan maintenance)
- <50: Poor (urgent action required)

**RUL Estimation:**
- Based on degradation trending
- Consider operating hours remaining
- Factor in maintenance history
- Estimate confidence level (high/medium/low)

## 8. Inspection Tools and Equipment

### Required Tools
- Infrared thermometer (±1.5°C accuracy)
- Handheld vibration meter or analyzer
- Digital multimeter with clamp attachment
- 500V insulation resistance tester (Megger)
- Laser alignment system (for monthly checks)
- Belt tension gauge
- Torque wrench set
- Borescope (for internal inspection)
- Ultrasonic thickness gauge

### Inspection Forms
- Daily operator log (paper or mobile app)
- Weekly technician checklist
- Monthly inspection report template
- Quarterly assessment spreadsheet
- Photo documentation (before/after)

## 9. Documentation and Trending

### Data Recording
All inspections must record:
- Date and time
- Equipment ID and description
- Inspector name
- Measurements taken (with units)
- Observations and findings
- Corrective actions recommended
- Follow-up required (Y/N)

### Trending Analysis
- Plot measurements over time (line chart)
- Identify upward or downward trends
- Calculate rate of change
- Predict when parameter will exceed threshold
- Use for RUL estimation

### Digital Systems
- Enter data into CMMS immediately after inspection
- Attach photos to work orders
- Generate automated alerts for out-of-range values
- Create dashboards for management visibility

## 10. Common Defects and Early Indicators

| Defect | Early Warning Signs | Inspection Finding | Action |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|--------|
| Bearing failure | Slight temperature rise, faint noise | Vibration >7 mm/s, temp >80°C | Replace bearing within 2 weeks |
| Motor winding fault | Slight overheating, imbalanced current | Insulation <2 MΩ, hot spot on IR | Schedule rewind, monitor closely |
| Misalignment | Low vibration at 2× RPM | Offset >0.5mm, coupling wear | Laser align, replace coupling if damaged |
| Belt wear | Occasional slippage, dust | Cracks, glazing, >10% elongation | Replace belt, check pulleys |
| Oil contamination | Slight darkening | Water >500 ppm, particles >ISO 18/16/13 | Change oil, investigate source |
| Loose connection | Warm to touch | Hot spot >40°C above ambient | Tighten, clean contact surface |
| Pump wear | Slight pressure drop | Flow <90% of baseline | Inspect impeller, check clearances |

## 11. Training and Competency

### Inspector Qualification
- Operators: Basic visual inspection training (4 hours)
- Technicians: Equipment-specific inspection training (16 hours)
- Reliability technicians: Vibration analysis Cat I, thermography Level 1

### Annual Refresher
- Review of inspection procedures
- New defect case studies
- Update on equipment changes
- Tool calibration and usage

## 12. Continuous Improvement
- Monthly review of inspection findings
- Identify most common defects
- Update inspection checklists based on learnings
- Reduce inspection time while improving quality
- Benchmark inspection compliance (target: >95%)
