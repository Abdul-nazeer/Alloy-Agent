# Emergency Equipment Breakdown Response Procedure

## 1. Purpose
This procedure defines the immediate response actions for unexpected equipment failures that impact production, safety, or operations.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all critical equipment breakdowns requiring emergency response including motors, compressors, pumps, conveyors, and auxiliary systems.

## 3. Emergency Classification

### Priority 1: Critical Emergency
**Definition**: Equipment failure causing immediate safety hazard or complete production stoppage

**Examples**:
- Fire or smoke from equipment
- Structural failure or collapse
- Fluid leak with safety risk (oil, hydraulic, chemical)
- Explosion or violent failure
- Personnel injury involving equipment

**Response Time**: Immediate (mobilize within 15 minutes)

### Priority 2: High Priority
**Definition**: Equipment failure stopping production line but no safety risk

**Examples**:
- Main mill motor failure
- Primary compressor failure
- Cooling system failure
- Critical pump failure
- Conveyor breakdown on main line

**Response Time**: Within 1 hour

### Priority 3: Medium Priority
**Definition**: Equipment failure with production workaround available

**Examples**:
- Auxiliary equipment failure
- Redundant system failure
- Non-critical conveyor stoppage

**Response Time**: Within 4 hours

## 4. Emergency Response Roles

### Emergency Response Team (ERT)
- **Team Leader**: Maintenance Supervisor
- **Technical Lead**: Senior Technician/Engineer
- **Safety Officer**: HSE Representative
- **Logistics Coordinator**: Spare Parts Controller
- **Documentation Lead**: Reliability Engineer

### On-Call Rotation
- Primary on-call: 24/7 availability
- Secondary backup: 30-minute response time
- Contact list updated weekly

## 5. Immediate Response Protocol (First 15 Minutes)

### Step 1: Safety First (0-5 minutes)
1. **Ensure Personnel Safety**
   - Evacuate immediate area if hazardous
   - Account for all personnel
   - Establish safety perimeter (minimum 10 meters)
   - Post warning signs/barricades

2. **Secure the Equipment**
   - Emergency stop activation
   - LOTO (Lock-Out/Tag-Out) procedures
   - Isolate electrical supply
   - Close fluid isolation valves
   - Verify zero energy state

3. **Assess Immediate Hazards**
   - Fire risk → Call fire brigade
   - Chemical/oil spill → Activate spill response
   - Structural damage → Evacuate building
   - No hazard → Proceed to diagnosis

### Step 2: Initial Assessment (5-10 minutes)
1. **Identify Failed Equipment**
   - Equipment ID and type
   - Operating conditions before failure
   - Symptoms observed (noise, smoke, vibration)
   - Error messages or alarms

2. **Document Initial Findings**
   - Take photos/videos of damage
   - Record witness statements
   - Note operating hours/cycles
   - Capture control panel readings

3. **Notify Response Team**
   - Alert maintenance supervisor
   - Call in ERT members
   - Contact equipment vendor if needed
   - Inform production manager

### Step 3: Quick Diagnosis (10-15 minutes)
1. **Visual Inspection**
   - Check for obvious damage (cracks, burns, leaks)
   - Inspect connections and mountings
   - Look for contamination or foreign objects
   - Examine surroundings for clues

2. **Classify Failure Type**
   - Mechanical (bearing, coupling, shaft)
   - Electrical (motor winding, connection, control)
   - Hydraulic/Pneumatic (seal, valve, cylinder)
   - Structural (frame, foundation, housing)

3. **Estimate Repair Complexity**
   - Simple fix: <2 hours (belt, connection, adjustment)
   - Moderate repair: 2-8 hours (bearing, coupling)
   - Major repair: >8 hours (motor rewind, component replacement)
   - Capital replacement: >24 hours (new equipment)

## 6. Emergency Repair Decision Tree

### Can Equipment Be Temporarily Bypassed?
**YES** → Implement workaround, schedule proper repair
- Activate redundant equipment
- Reroute process flow
- Use manual operation mode
- Accept reduced capacity

**NO** → Proceed with emergency repair

### Are Spare Parts Available On-Site?
**YES** → Proceed with immediate repair
- Retrieve parts from warehouse
- Verify part compatibility
- Prepare tools and equipment

**NO** → Check emergency procurement options
- Contact vendor for emergency delivery
- Check nearby plants for loan/borrow
- Consider temporary repair with available materials
- Evaluate priority shipping (24-hour courier)

### Can Repair Be Done On-Site?
**YES** → Mobilize technicians and tools
- Set up work area
- Arrange lighting and power
- Prepare lifting equipment if needed
- Ensure access and clearance

**NO** → Plan for removal and external repair
- Arrange rigging and transportation
- Contact repair vendor with urgency
- Prepare replacement or spare unit
- Schedule installation after repair

## 7. Emergency Repair Execution

### Pre-Repair Checklist
- [ ] Work permit issued and signed
- [ ] LOTO verified by safety officer
- [ ] Spare parts verified and on-site
- [ ] Tools and equipment prepared
- [ ] Technicians briefed on task
- [ ] Production informed of timeline
- [ ] Lighting and ventilation adequate
- [ ] Emergency contacts posted

### During Repair - Safety Protocols
- Maintain LOTO at all times
- Use proper PPE (gloves, eye protection, hearing protection)
- No lone working - minimum 2 persons
- Take breaks every 2 hours for long repairs
- Update status to control room every 30 minutes
- Document all steps with photos
- Report any complications immediately

### Quality Checkpoints
- Verify part orientation and alignment
- Torque bolts to specification (use torque wrench)
- Check clearances and fits
- Lubricate as per procedure
- Inspect for foreign objects before closing
- Verify electrical connections and polarity

### Post-Repair Testing (Before Production)
1. **Bump Test** (if motor)
   - Momentary start to verify rotation direction
   - Check for unusual noise or vibration

2. **No-Load Run Test**
   - Run for 15 minutes without load
   - Monitor temperature, vibration, current
   - Listen for abnormal sounds
   - Check for leaks or unusual smells

3. **Load Test**
   - Gradually apply load to 50%, then 75%, then 100%
   - Monitor all parameters at each step
   - Verify operation within normal ranges
   - Record baseline readings for future reference

4. **Final Verification**
   - All guards and covers reinstalled
   - Area cleaned and tools removed
   - LOTO removed following procedure
   - Equipment returned to production control

## 10. Temporary Fixes and Workarounds

### When Acceptable
- Waiting for permanent parts (delivery <7 days)
- Reduced capacity acceptable to production
- Safety not compromised
- Monitored continuously until proper repair

### Common Temporary Solutions
- Bearing with damage → Run at reduced speed with frequent monitoring
- Leaking seal → Use drip pan and frequent top-up
- Loose mounting → Additional temporary bracing
- Damaged coupling → Replace with flexible coupling as interim

### Mandatory for Temporary Fixes
- Document as temporary (red tag on equipment)
- Set maximum run time or cycles
- Schedule proper repair within 7 days
- Increased monitoring frequency
- Clear responsibility for follow-up

## 11. Root Cause Analysis (Within 48 Hours)

### Required for All Priority 1 & 2 Failures
- Conduct formal RCA meeting
- Use 5-Why or Fishbone analysis
- Identify contributing factors
- Determine corrective and preventive actions
- Update equipment maintenance plan
- Share lessons learned with team

### RCA Output
- Detailed failure report
- Timeline of events
- Root cause determination
- Corrective actions (immediate)
- Preventive actions (long-term)
- Responsible persons and deadlines
- Estimated cost impact (downtime + repair)

## 12. Documentation Requirements

### Emergency Response Log
- Date/time of failure
- Equipment ID and description
- Failure symptoms and observations
- Response team members
- Actions taken (timeline)
- Parts replaced (with part numbers)
- Total downtime duration
- Root cause summary

### Cost Tracking
- Labor hours (regular and overtime)
- Parts and materials cost
- External vendor charges
- Production loss estimate
- Total emergency response cost

### CMMS Update
- Close work order with details
- Update equipment history
- Record parts consumed
- Set follow-up tasks
- Update preventive maintenance schedule

## 13. Performance Metrics

### Emergency Response KPIs
- **Mean Time To Respond (MTTR)**: <30 minutes for Priority 1
- **Mean Time To Repair (MTTR)**: <8 hours for Priority 2
- **First-Time Fix Rate**: >85%
- **Emergency Downtime**: <5% of total operating time
- **Repeat Failure Rate**: <10% within 30 days
