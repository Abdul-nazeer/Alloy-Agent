# Bearing Maintenance and Lubrication Procedure

## 1. Purpose
This procedure establishes the standard process for bearing inspection, lubrication, and maintenance in rotating equipment including motors, compressors, and conveyor systems.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all ball bearings, roller bearings, and thrust bearings in steel plant equipment.

## 3. Frequency
- **Lubrication**: Every 500 operating hours or monthly (whichever comes first)
- **Inspection**: Every 1,000 operating hours or quarterly
- **Replacement**: Based on vibration analysis and condition monitoring

## 4. Required Tools and Materials
- Grease gun with NLGI Grade 2 lithium-based grease
- Ultrasonic thickness gauge
- Vibration analyzer
- Infrared thermometer
- Cleaning solvent and lint-free cloth
- Bearing puller kit
- Protective equipment (gloves, safety glasses)

## 5. Safety Precautions
- Lock-out/Tag-out (LOTO) equipment before maintenance
- Ensure equipment is at ambient temperature
- Use proper PPE at all times
- Verify zero energy state before work begins

## 6. Lubrication Procedure

### 6.1 Pre-Lubrication Inspection
1. Check bearing temperature (normal range: 40-80°C)
2. Listen for abnormal noise (grinding, squealing)
3. Measure vibration levels (acceptable: <2.5 mm/s RMS)
4. Inspect grease relief ports for blockage

### 6.2 Grease Application
1. Clean grease fitting with lint-free cloth
2. Apply grease slowly using grease gun
3. Purge old grease until fresh grease appears
4. Apply grease amount based on bearing size:
   - Small bearings (<50mm): 10-15 grams
   - Medium bearings (50-100mm): 20-30 grams
   - Large bearings (>100mm): 40-60 grams
5. Run equipment for 10-15 minutes to distribute grease
6. Check for excessive grease discharge

### 6.3 Post-Lubrication Verification
1. Monitor temperature rise (should not exceed 10°C above baseline)
2. Verify smooth operation with no unusual noise
3. Record lubrication date and grease quantity used

## 7. Inspection Criteria

### 7.1 Visual Inspection
- Check for grease leakage or contamination
- Inspect seals for wear or damage
- Look for rust, corrosion, or discoloration
- Examine housing for cracks or deformation

### 7.2 Vibration Analysis Thresholds
- **Good**: <2.5 mm/s RMS
- **Acceptable**: 2.5-7.0 mm/s RMS
- **Unsatisfactory**: 7.0-18.0 mm/s RMS (Plan maintenance)
- **Unacceptable**: >18.0 mm/s RMS (Immediate shutdown)

### 7.3 Temperature Monitoring
- **Normal**: 40-80°C
- **Warning**: 80-100°C (Investigate within 24 hours)
- **Critical**: >100°C (Immediate shutdown required)

## 8. Bearing Replacement Criteria
Replace bearing immediately if:
- Vibration exceeds 18.0 mm/s RMS
- Temperature exceeds 100°C
- Audible grinding or metal-to-metal contact
- Visible cracks or spalling on races
- Excessive play or axial movement
- Grease contamination with metal particles

## 9. Common Failure Modes and Root Causes

### 9.1 Overheating
- **Cause**: Insufficient lubrication, over-lubrication, misalignment
- **Action**: Check grease quantity, verify alignment, inspect for bearing damage

### 9.2 High Vibration
- **Cause**: Bearing wear, unbalance, misalignment, loose mounting
- **Action**: Perform vibration analysis, check mounting bolts, inspect coupling

### 9.3 Premature Failure
- **Cause**: Contamination, improper installation, overload
- **Action**: Improve sealing, verify installation procedure, check load calculations

## 10. Documentation Requirements
Record the following in maintenance log:
- Date and time of service
- Equipment ID and bearing location
- Lubrication quantity and grease type
- Temperature and vibration readings
- Visual inspection findings
- Technician name and signature
- Next scheduled maintenance date

## 11. Spare Parts Inventory
Maintain minimum stock of:
- Common bearing sizes (6205, 6305, 6310, 22220)
- NLGI Grade 2 lithium grease (50kg drum)
- Bearing seals and retaining rings
- Grease fittings and relief plugs
